stuck

adj./v.

1.unable to move or to be moved

eg: The weels were stuck in the mud.

2. in an unpleasant situation or place that you cannot escape from

eg.

we were stuck in traffic for over an hour.
I hate being stuck at home all day.

3. ~ on sth. unable to answer or understand sth

eg: I’ll help you if you’re stuck.

4. ~ for sth. not knowing what to do in a parcular

eg. I’ve never known him to be stuck for words before

5. ~ with sb./sth. unable to get rid of sb./sth. that you do not want

eg. I was stuck with him for the whole journey

despite

prep.

1. used to show that sth happened or is true although sth else might have happened to prevent it

syn. in spite of: Her voice was shaking despite all her efforts to control it
eg. Despite applying for hundreds of jobs, he is still out of work.

2 despite yourself: used to show that sb did not intent to do the thing mentioned

syn: in spite of
eg: He had to laugh despite himself.

bend

verb.

1. (especially of sb’s body or head) to lean, or make sth lean, in a particular direction

eg.

  • +adv./prep. field of poppies bending in the wind; She suddenly bent over, clutching her stomach.
  • ~ sth (+ adv./prep.) He bent his head and kissed her.

    2. if you bend your arm, leg, etc. or if it bends , you move it so that it is no longer straight

    ~ sth bend your knees, keeping your back straight

    Lie flat and let your kness bend.

    3. ~ sth to : to force sth that was straight into an angle or a curve

    eg.

  • The knives were bent out of shappe.
  • He bent the wire into the shape of a square.

    4. to change direction to form a curve or an angle; to make sth change direction in this way.

    eg. ~ sth Glass and water both bend light

    phrase

  • bend sb’s ear(about sth) : to talk to sb a lot about sth, especially about a problem that you have
  • bend your mind/effort to sth : to think very hard about or put a lot of effort into one particular thing
  • bend the truth : to say sth that is not completely true
  • on bended kness: if you ask for sth on bended knees, you as it in a very anxious and/or humble way(= showing you think you are less important than the person you are asking)
  • bend to sth: to be forced or persuaded to do what sb wants or to accept their opinion.
  • bend sb to sth: to force or persuade sb to do what you want or to accept your opinions: He manipulates people and tries to bend them to his will.(= make them do what he wants)

noun

1. (C) a curve or turn, especially in a road or river

2. the bends: (pl.) severe pain and difficulty in breathing experienced by a diver who comes back to the surface of the water too quickly. SYN: decompression sickness

Idioms

  • round the bend/twist(north American English around the bend): crazy
  • drive sb round the bend: to annoy sb. His attitude drives me found the bend

  • The mark for their products is shrinking.
  • Our planet has shrunk.

    3. + adv/prep. to move back or away from sth because you are fightened or shocked

    SYN:cower He shrank back against the wall as he heard them approaching

anxious

adj:

1.felling worried or nevous

EG

  • The bus was late and Sue began to get anxious.

    phrase

  • ~ about sth: I felt very anxious and depressed abot the future.
  • ~ for sb: parents are naturally anxious for their children.

    2. causing worry; showing worry

    The family faces another anxious wait for news

    3.wanting sth very much

    phrase

  • ~ to do sth: She was anxious to finish school and get a job.
  • ~ for sth: There are plenty of graduates anxious for work.
  • ~ for sb to do sth: I’m anxious that he should meet her father.

    anxiety

    noun.

    1.~(about/over sth) the state of feeling nervous or worried that sth bad is going to happen: acute/intense/deep anxiety

    EG: Some hospital patients experience high levels of anxiety.

    2.(psychology) a mental heath problem that causes sb to worry so much that it has a very negative effect on their daily life

    EG: Some people are feeling the pressure and suffering from anxiety and depression.

    3. a worry or fear about sth: if you’re worry about your health, share your anxieties with your doctor.

    4. a strong feeling of wanting to do sth or of wanting sth to happen.

    phrase

  • ~ to do sth: the candidate’s anxiety to win the vote.
  • ~ for sth: the people’s anxiety for the war to end.

    bias

    noun.

    1. a strong feeling in favour of or against one group of people, or one side in an argument, often not based on fair judgement: accusations of political bias in news programmes (= that reports are unfair and show favour to one political party)

    EG

  1. Employers must cinsuder all candidates impartially and without bias
  2. Some institutions still have a strong bias against women.
  3. The article examines gender bias in our schools.

    2. an interest in one thing more than others; a special ability: the course has a strong practical bias.

    3. the fact that the results of research or an experiment are not accurate because a particular factor has not been considered when collecting the information.

    EG: If a response rate is low, the risk of bias in the finding will be great.

    4. the bias of a piece of cloth is an edge cut.

    verb.

    1. ~ sb/sth (against/towards/in favour of sb/sth) to unfairly influence sb’s opinions or decisions

    SYN: prejudice: the newspapers have biased people against her.

    2. ~ sth: to have an effet on the results of research or an experiment contained an error which could bias the results.
